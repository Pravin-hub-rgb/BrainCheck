### Sub-section 7.1: Languages & Linguistic Diversity (Questions 1-5)

1. **How many languages are listed in the Eighth Schedule of the Constitution (official/scheduled languages)?**  
   Answer: 22 (as of latest; includes Assamese, Bengali, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Kashmiri, Konkani, Malayalam, Manipuri, Marathi, Nepali, Odia, Punjabi, Sanskrit, Sindhi, Tamil, Telugu, Urdu, Bodo, Santali, Maithili, Dogri).

2. **Which is the link language for official purposes alongside English?**  
   Answer: Hindi (Devanagari script; English for certain Union purposes).

3. **Approximately how many languages and dialects are spoken in India?**  
   Answer: Over 19,500 mother tongues/dialects (Census data); around 121 major languages.

4. **Which state has the most number of scheduled languages spoken?**  
   Answer: Varies, but Northeast states like Assam/Manipur are highly multilingual; India overall has immense diversity.

5. **What is the significance of Sanskrit in Indian culture?**  
   Answer: Classical language of ancient texts (Vedas, Upanishads); mother of many Indo-Aryan languages; listed in Eighth Schedule.

### Sub-section 7.2: Classical & Folk Arts (Questions 6-10)

6. **How many classical dance forms are officially recognized by Sangeet Natak Akademi?**  
   Answer: 8 (Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Kathakali, Kuchipudi, Odissi, Manipuri, Mohiniyattam, Sattriya).

7. **Which classical dance originates from Kerala and features elaborate makeup/costumes?**  
   Answer: Kathakali.

8. **Which classical dance is from Uttar Pradesh/Rajasthan and tells stories through footwork and expressions?**  
   Answer: Kathak.

9. **Name one major folk dance from Punjab celebrated during Baisakhi.**  
   Answer: Bhangra (energetic, with dhol; often includes Giddha for women).

10. **Which folk theatre form from Maharashtra uses song, dance, and satire?**  
    Answer: Tamasha (or Lavani dance element).

### Sub-section 7.3: Major Festivals & Their Significance (Questions 11-18)

11. **What is Diwali (Deepavali), and why is it celebrated?**  
    Answer: Festival of Lights (October/November); victory of light over darkness, good over evil (Lord Rama's return to Ayodhya); also Lakshmi puja for prosperity; inscribed as UNESCO Intangible Heritage in 2025.

12. **What is Holi, and what does it symbolize?**  
    Answer: Festival of Colors (March); arrival of spring, victory of good (Holika-Prahlad story); forgiveness, love, and joy through gulal throwing.

13. **What is Durga Puja/Dussehra, and its key significance?**  
    Answer: Navratri/Dussehra (September/October); Durga's victory over Mahishasura; in Bengal, grand pandals; burning of Ravana effigies symbolizing good over evil.

14. **What is Ganesh Chaturthi?**  
    Answer: Birthday of Lord Ganesha (August/September); 10-day festival with idols immersion; remover of obstacles, wisdom.

15. **What is Eid-ul-Fitr, and when is it celebrated?**  
    Answer: End of Ramadan (Islamic fasting month); marks joy, charity, family feasts.

16. **What is Onam in Kerala?**  
    Answer: Harvest festival (August/September); welcomes King Mahabali; snake boat races, pookalam (flower rangoli), sadhya feast.

17. **What is the Kumbh Mela?**  
    Answer: World's largest religious gathering (every 12 years, rotates between Prayagraj, Haridwar, Nashik, Ujjain); bathing in sacred rivers for purification; UNESCO Intangible Heritage since 2017.

18. **What is Pongal in Tamil Nadu?**  
    Answer: Harvest thanksgiving (January); boiling rice in milk; thanks to Sun God and cattle.

### Sub-section 7.4: Heritage & Traditions (Questions 19-25)

19. **How many UNESCO World Heritage Sites does India have as of 2026?**  
    Answer: 44 (36 cultural, 7 natural, 1 mixed; latest addition: Maratha Military Landscapes in 2025).

20. **Name India's most famous UNESCO site symbolizing love.**  
    Answer: Taj Mahal (Agra; Mughal architecture, 1983 inscription).

21. **Which UNESCO site is a living pilgrimage and festival (intangible)?**  
    Answer: Kumbh Mela (inscribed 2017).

22. **What is Yoga's significance, and when was it inscribed as UNESCO Intangible Heritage?**  
    Answer: Ancient Indian practice for physical/mental well-being; inscribed 2016; International Yoga Day: June 21.

23. **Which UNESCO intangible element from Gujarat is a circular folk dance?**  
    Answer: Garba (inscribed 2023; performed during Navratri).

24. **Name one tradition that reflects India's arranged marriage custom.**  
    Answer: Family involvement in matchmaking; horoscope matching common in many communities.
