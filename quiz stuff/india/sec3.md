### Sub-section 3.1: Pre-Independence & Freedom Struggle Buildup (Questions 1-15)

1. **When did the Revolt of 1857 (First War of Independence) begin?**  
   Answer: May 10, 1857 (started in Meerut; spread across northern India).

2. **Who is considered the first martyr of the 1857 Revolt?**  
   Answer: Mangal Pandey (fired at British officers on March 29, 1857, in Barrackpore).

3. **Which queen led the resistance in Jhansi during 1857?**  
   Answer: Rani Lakshmibai.

4. **When was the Indian National Congress (INC) founded?**  
   Answer: December 28, 1885 (in Bombay; first president: W.C. Bonnerjee).

5. **Who partitioned Bengal in 1905, sparking the Swadeshi Movement?**  
   Answer: Lord Curzon (partition annulled in 1911).

6. **What was the main slogan of the Swadeshi Movement?**  
   Answer: Boycott foreign goods; promote Indian (swadeshi) products.

7. **When was the Muslim League formed?**  
   Answer: December 30, 1906 (in Dhaka).

8. **What was the Lucknow Pact (1916)?**  
   Answer: Agreement between INC and Muslim League for joint demands (separate electorates, self-rule).

9. **Which act introduced dyarchy in provinces?**  
   Answer: Government of India Act, 1919 (Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms).

10. **What tragic event occurred on April 13, 1919, in Amritsar?**  
    Answer: Jallianwala Bagh Massacre (General Dyer ordered firing; hundreds killed).

11. **Who launched the Non-Cooperation Movement in 1920?**  
    Answer: Mahatma Gandhi (withdrew after Chauri Chaura incident in 1922).

12. **What was the Chauri Chaura incident?**  
    Answer: February 4, 1922 â€“ mob violence led Gandhi to suspend Non-Cooperation.

13. **When did Gandhi start the Civil Disobedience Movement with the Salt March?**  
    Answer: March 12, 1930 (Dandi March; broke salt law on April 6, 1930).

14. **What was the demand at the Lahore Session of INC in 1929?**  
    Answer: Purna Swaraj (complete independence); January 26 declared Independence Day.

15. **Who gave the "Do or Die" slogan during Quit India Movement?**  
    Answer: Mahatma Gandhi (August 8, 1942; "Quit India" resolution).
