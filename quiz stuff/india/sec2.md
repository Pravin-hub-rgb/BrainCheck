**Section 2: Important Laws, Rights & Everyday Citizen Knowledge** (now expanded to ~30 questions).

This focuses on **practical, day-to-day laws** that affect ordinary citizensâ€”things like police interactions, traffic rules, women's rights, consumer protection, marriage/family laws, RTI, data privacy, and recent updates (e.g., new criminal laws from 2024, DPDPA 2023). I've included timeless essentials plus some 2024-2025 relevant ones for current awareness.

Questions are straightforward Q&A format, with accurate answers based on key acts and common knowledge.

1. **Can a police officer refuse to register an FIR (First Information Report)?**  
   Answer: No. Refusal is punishable under Section 166A of the Indian Penal Code (or equivalent in Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023). You can approach a higher officer or magistrate if refused.

2. **What is the legal blood alcohol limit for driving in India?**  
   Answer: 30 mg of alcohol per 100 ml of blood (Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Section 185; remains the same post-2024 updates).

3. **Is it mandatory to wear a helmet while riding a two-wheeler?**  
   Answer: Yes, for both rider and pillion (Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Section 129; fine for non-compliance).

4. **What is the maximum number of people allowed on a two-wheeler (excluding child)?**  
   Answer: Two adults (rider + one pillion); children under a certain age may be allowed extra in some rules, but generally limited.

5. **Under which law can women not be arrested after sunset and before sunrise (except in exceptional cases)?**  
   Answer: Section 46 of the Code of Criminal Procedure (now Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita 2023 has similar provisions with safeguards).

6. **Can unmarried adult couples stay together in hotels?**  
   Answer: Yes, as long as both are consenting adults; no law prohibits it (Supreme Court rulings protect privacy under Article 21).

7. **Is live-in relationship legally recognized in India?**  
   Answer: Yes, protected under Article 21 (right to life and liberty); partners can claim maintenance in some cases (Supreme Court judgments).

8. **What is the minimum legal age for marriage for women and men in India?**  
   Answer: Women: 18 years; Men: 21 years (Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006; no change as of 2025).

9. **Can a divorce petition be filed within one year of marriage?**  
   Answer: Generally no (Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, Section 14; exceptions for exceptional hardship/cruelty).

10. **What compensation can be claimed for a gas cylinder explosion accident?**  
    Answer: Up to â‚¹50 lakh or more under the Explosives Act, 1884, and Public Liability Insurance Act (common claim via district authorities).

11. **What is the Right to Information (RTI) Act, and who can file an RTI?**  
    Answer: RTI Act, 2005; any Indian citizen can seek information from public authorities (response within 30 days; fee â‚¹10).

12. **What is the Right to Education (RTE)?**  
    Answer: Free and compulsory education for children aged 6-14 (Article 21A + RTE Act, 2009).

13. **Is public display of affection (PDA) illegal in India?**  
    Answer: Not outright illegal, but obscene acts in public can be punished under Section 294 of IPC (or Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita equivalent).

14. **What is the POSH Act?**  
    Answer: Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013; mandates internal complaints committee.

15. **Under which section is dowry prohibited?**  
    Answer: Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961; also Section 498A (cruelty by husband/relatives; now under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita).

16. **What right allows free use of washrooms in hotels/restaurants?**  
    Answer: Indian Sarais Act, 1887 (common traveler right; often enforced via consumer forums).

17. **Who provides free legal aid to poor citizens?**  
    Answer: Under Article 39A and Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987; through District Legal Services Authority.

18. **What is the right to remain silent during police questioning?**  
    Answer: Yes, Article 20(3): No accused can be compelled to be a witness against themselves.

19. **What is the Consumer Protection Act, 2019?**  
    Answer: Protects against unfair trade practices, defective goods, misleading ads; allows e-commerce complaints too.

20. **What is the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA), 2023?**  
    Answer: India's first comprehensive data privacy law; gives citizens rights over personal data (consent, correction, erasure).

21. **When did the new criminal laws (BNS, BNSS, BSA) replace IPC, CrPC, Evidence Act?**  
    Answer: July 1, 2024 (Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam).

22. **What change did the new laws bring to zero FIR?**  
    Answer: Zero FIR can be filed anywhere (no jurisdictional limit); mandatory to transfer to proper police station.

23. **Is helmet mandatory for women on two-wheelers?**  
    Answer: Yes, same as men (no exemption except in some state rules for religious headgear).

24. **What is Section 498A related to?**  
    Answer: Cruelty by husband or relatives (cognizable offense; often used in domestic violence cases).

25. **Can you file a complaint against misleading advertisements?**  
    Answer: Yes, under Consumer Protection Act, 2019; Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA) handles it.

26. **What is the age for voting in India?**  
    Answer: 18 years (as per Constitution and Representation of the People Act).

27. **Is triple talaq illegal?**  
    Answer: Yes, Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act, 2019; criminal offense.

28. **What right protects against arbitrary arrest?**  
    Answer: Article 22: Right to be informed of grounds of arrest, consult lawyer, produced before magistrate within 24 hours.

29. **Can citizens seek information about private companies under RTI?**  
    Answer: No, only public authorities; but if substantially financed by government, sometimes yes.

30. **What is a key feature of the new criminal laws for women?**  
    Answer: Timelines for investigation/trial in crimes against women/children; mandatory audio-video recording of searches.
