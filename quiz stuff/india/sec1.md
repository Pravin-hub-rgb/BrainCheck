### Section 1: Indian Constitution and Fundamental Rights & Duties (30 Questions)

1. **When was the Constitution of India adopted?**  
   Answer: November 26, 1949 (hence Constitution Day is celebrated on this date).

2. **When did the Constitution come into force?**  
   Answer: January 26, 1950 (Republic Day).

3. **Who was the Chairman of the Drafting Committee of the Constitution?**  
   Answer: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar.

4. **How many Articles are there in the original Constitution?**  
   Answer: 395 (now more due to amendments; currently around 470+ effective articles).

5. **What are the words added to the Preamble by the 42nd Amendment in 1976?**  
   Answer: Socialist, Secular, and Integrity (now: Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic).

6. **What are the six Fundamental Rights guaranteed by the Constitution?**  
   Answer: (1) Right to Equality (2) Right to Freedom (3) Right against Exploitation (4) Right to Freedom of Religion (5) Cultural and Educational Rights (6) Right to Constitutional Remedies.

7. **Which Articles cover the Right to Equality?**  
   Answer: Articles 14â€“18 (equality before law, prohibition of discrimination, equality of opportunity, abolition of untouchability, abolition of titles).

8. **What does Article 14 guarantee?**  
   Answer: Equality before the law and equal protection of the laws.

9. **Which Fundamental Right includes freedom of speech and expression, assembly, association, movement, residence, and profession?**  
   Answer: Right to Freedom (Article 19).

10. **Which Article protects the Right to Life and Personal Liberty?**  
    Answer: Article 21 (widely interpreted to include dignity, privacy, education, health, etc.).

11. **Which Fundamental Right prohibits traffic in human beings, forced labour, and child labour in hazardous jobs?**  
    Answer: Right against Exploitation (Articles 23â€“24).

12. **What is the age below which children cannot be employed in factories/mines/hazardous work?**  
    Answer: 14 years (Article 24).

13. **Which Articles guarantee freedom of religion (profession, practice, propagation, and management of religious affairs)?**  
    Answer: Articles 25â€“28.

14. **Which Fundamental Right allows minorities to establish and administer educational institutions?**  
    Answer: Cultural and Educational Rights (Articles 29â€“30).

15. **What is the "heart and soul" of the Constitution according to Dr. Ambedkar?**  
    Answer: Article 32 (Right to Constitutional Remedies â€“ right to approach Supreme Court for enforcement of Fundamental Rights).

16. **Can Fundamental Rights be suspended? Under what condition?**  
    Answer: Yes, during a national emergency proclaimed under Article 352 (but Articles 20 and 21 cannot be suspended).

17. **Which right was originally a Fundamental Right but made a legal right by the 44th Amendment in 1978?**  
    Answer: Right to Property (now Article 300A, not a Fundamental Right).

18. **How many Fundamental Duties are listed in the Constitution?**  
    Answer: 11 (added by 42nd Amendment in 1976; one more added later).

19. **Under which Article are Fundamental Duties mentioned?**  
    Answer: Article 51A (Part IVA).

20. **Name the first Fundamental Duty.**  
    Answer: To abide by the Constitution and respect its ideals and institutions, the National Flag and the National Anthem.

21. **Which Fundamental Duty asks citizens to promote harmony and brotherhood, and renounce practices derogatory to the dignity of women?**  
    Answer: Duty (e): To promote harmony... and to renounce practices derogatory to the dignity of women.

22. **Which duty relates to protecting the environment?**  
    Answer: Duty (g): To protect and improve the natural environment including forests, lakes, rivers and wildlife, and to have compassion for living creatures.

23. **Which Fundamental Duty was added by the 86th Amendment in 2002 (though not yet fully enforced)?**  
    Answer: Duty (k): Parents/guardians to provide education opportunities to children aged 6â€“14 years.

24. **Which amendment introduced the words 'Socialist' and 'Secular' in the Preamble?**  
    Answer: 42nd Amendment (1976).

25. **Which amendment is known as the "Mini-Constitution" due to many changes?**  
    Answer: 42nd Amendment (1976).

26. **How many Schedules are there in the Constitution now?**  
    Answer: 12 (originally 8).

27. **Which Schedule lists the languages recognized in India?**  
    Answer: Eighth Schedule (22 official languages).

28. **What is the significance of the 73rd and 74th Amendments?**  
    Answer: 73rd for Panchayati Raj (rural local self-government); 74th for Municipalities (urban local self-government).

29. **Which Article deals with the abolition of untouchability?**  
    Answer: Article 17.

30. **Who can enforce Fundamental Rights through writs like habeas corpus, mandamus, etc.?**  
    Answer: Supreme Court (Article 32) and High Courts (Article 226).
