**Section 5: Government Structure & Polity** (expanded to ~30 questions).

This covers the **federal structure**, **Union executive** (President, PM, Council of Ministers), **Parliament** (Lok Sabha & Rajya Sabha), **state governance**, **local self-government** (73rd & 74th Amendments), **elections**, and key constitutional articles. Questions are based on the Constitution, standard polity facts, and current structure (as of 2026: Lok Sabha 543 elected seats; Rajya Sabha 245 total â€“ 233 elected + 12 nominated). I've grouped them into sub-themes.

### Sub-section 5.1: Federal Structure & Basic Polity (Questions 1-8)

1. **What type of government does India have?**  
   Answer: Federal parliamentary democratic republic (sovereign, socialist, secular as per Preamble).

2. **How is power divided in India's federal system?**  
   Answer: Between Union (Centre) and States via Seventh Schedule: Union List (97 subjects), State List (61), Concurrent List (52); residuary powers with Union.

3. **Who is the head of state in India?**  
   Answer: The President (ceremonial; real executive power with PM and Council of Ministers).

4. **Who is the head of government?**  
   Answer: The Prime Minister (leads the Council of Ministers; accountable to Lok Sabha).

5. **What are the three branches of government in India?**  
   Answer: Executive (President + Council of Ministers), Legislature (Parliament), Judiciary (Supreme Court + High Courts).

6. **Which part of the Constitution deals with the Union?**  
   Answer: Part V (Articles 52â€“151: President, VP, Council of Ministers, Parliament, etc.).

7. **What is the significance of Article 1?**  
   Answer: India, that is Bharat, shall be a Union of States (emphasizes federal but unitary bias).

8. **How many tiers of government exist in India?**  
   Answer: Three: Union (Centre), State, and Local (Panchayati Raj/Municipalities via 73rd & 74th Amendments).

### Sub-section 5.2: President & Executive Powers (Questions 9-15)

9. **Under which Article is the President mentioned?**  
   Answer: Article 52: There shall be a President of India.

10. **How is the President elected?**  
    Answer: By an electoral college (elected MPs + elected MLAs of states/UTs) via proportional representation with single transferable vote (Article 54â€“55).

11. **What is the term of the President?**  
    Answer: 5 years (can be re-elected; no term limit).

12. **Who appoints the Prime Minister?**  
    Answer: The President (usually the leader of the majority party/coalition in Lok Sabha â€“ Article 75).

13. **Under which Article must the President act on the aid and advice of the Council of Ministers?**  
    Answer: Article 74 (PM heads the Council; President bound by advice, except in rare discretionary cases).

14. **What are some key powers of the President?**  
    Answer: Appoint PM/Ministers, Governors, CJI, Election Commissioners; promulgate ordinances (Article 123); pardon (Article 72); supreme commander of armed forces; emergency proclamation (Article 352/356/360).

15. **Can the President dissolve Lok Sabha?**  
    Answer: Yes, on the advice of the Prime Minister (or if no stable government forms).

### Sub-section 5.3: Parliament â€“ Lok Sabha & Rajya Sabha (Questions 16-22)

16. **What is the bicameral Parliament of India called?**  
    Answer: Consists of President + Lok Sabha (House of the People) + Rajya Sabha (Council of States).

17. **How many seats are there in Lok Sabha currently?**  
    Answer: 543 elected (maximum 552 as per Constitution; frozen based on 1971 census till after 2026 delimitation).

18. **How many members in Rajya Sabha?**  
    Answer: 245 total (233 elected by state assemblies + 12 nominated by President for expertise in arts/science/literature).

19. **What is the term of Lok Sabha?**  
    Answer: 5 years (can be dissolved earlier; extendable during emergency).

20. **Rajya Sabha is a permanent house â€“ true or false?**  
    Answer: True (not subject to dissolution; 1/3 members retire every 2 years; 6-year term).

21. **Who presides over Lok Sabha?**  
    Answer: The Speaker (elected from among members).

22. **Who is the ex-officio Chairman of Rajya Sabha?**  
    Answer: The Vice-President of India.

### Sub-section 5.4: Council of Ministers, PM & Key Institutions (Questions 23-26)

23. **Who appoints other Ministers?**  
    Answer: President on the advice of the Prime Minister (Article 75).

24. **What is the maximum size of the Council of Ministers?**  
    Answer: 15% of the total strength of Lok Sabha (91st Amendment, 2003).

25. **Who is the chief advisor to the President on Union matters?**  
    Answer: The Prime Minister (duties under Article 78: communicate decisions, furnish info).

26. **What is CAG?**  
    Answer: Comptroller and Auditor General (Article 148: independent auditor of government accounts; appointed by President).

### Sub-section 5.5: Local Self-Government (73rd & 74th Amendments) (Questions 27-30)

27. **Which amendment introduced Panchayati Raj Institutions (rural local self-government)?**  
    Answer: 73rd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992 (added Part IX & Eleventh Schedule with 29 subjects).

28. **What is the three-tier system under 73rd Amendment?**  
    Answer: Gram Panchayat (village), Panchayat Samiti/Block (intermediate), Zila Parishad (district); mandatory except in small states (<20 lakh population).

29. **Which amendment deals with Municipalities (urban local bodies)?**  
    Answer: 74th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992 (added Part IXA & Twelfth Schedule with 18 subjects).

30. **What are the three types of Municipalities under 74th Amendment?**  
    Answer: Nagar Panchayat (transitional rural-urban areas), Municipal Council (smaller urban), Municipal Corporation (larger urban); direct elections, reservations, State Election Commission, Finance Commission.
